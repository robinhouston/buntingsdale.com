Information for wedding guests

If you’re reading this, that’s probably because you’re coming to our wedding. Which is so lovely and kind of you.

The important part: it’s at 2:30 pm on the 28 November 2015 at Christ Church Little Drayton, Buntingsdale Road, Shropshire TF9 1LT, and afterwards at Buntingsdale Hall. Which is just up the drive. We’re hoping to walk back unless the weather is really, really awful.

We’ll update this website over the next few weeks as we get more organised or change our minds about things, so do check again nearer the time.

Where to go

Christ Church Little Drayton is near Market Drayton on Buntingsdale Road, on the left-hand side as you come from Market Drayton. Buntingsdale Hall is a mile further down the road.

Getting there

If coming by car, it should be possible to park along the road by the church. We’re hoping we’ll be able to use the field opposite for additional parking too.

Public transport is more complicated. If you can get a lift with someone who is coming by car, that may be a better option.

There are buses from Stoke-on-Trent and Shrewsbury, but neither of them run on Sunday.

The closest railway station is Prees, about ten miles away, which will be connected by rail-replacement bus to Crewe and Shrewsbury. But you'll need to book a taxi, (and if by then it's back to trains, also tell the train driver before you get on you want to stop there; on the way back, just stick your hand out to ask the train to stop (it's a request stop). Be ready to jump down off the train/jump up - there is a wooden block to help).

Children

It would be lovely to see your children.

If your children get bored and restless during the service, there is a playground on the other side of the road (and also a pub. Just saying).

At the reception there will be curry or something. We’ll get some ham and cheese for children who don’t like curry, which you can ask for in the kitchen. You may like to bring other food for your children too: I imagine the kitchen will be in chaos, so if you could be really kind and bring cold food that would be brilliant.

Mattie (5) has described the house as ‘a bit hurty’; I'm afraid I concur. The house is sadly not terribly child friendly, with a big deep open pond with sloping sides, open fires, candles, steep stairs with holes in the bannisters and a rather rapacious wolf. It's amazing any children survive, so just to say you will need to keep an eye on them at the reception. If it all gets too much, there's a room and a kitchen above the dining room with a chest of toys/DVDs. Maybe they can lie on the floor up there. Sadly there won't be anyone able to look after your children for you. Actually, maybe there will. We’re working on that.

We've also asked way too many people, as we obviously couldn't bear to not ask any one of you, so if you could let those who would probably be happier lying on the floor or sitting on a lap do so (and not take up an adult chair) that would really help in the church and supper.

Presents

While we thought we didn’t need anything, in terms of presents, I (Victoria) forgot about my burning desire for a biomass boiler; it didn’t seem in the same class as a fondue set. I’ve bored most of you with it for ages, and I imagine you will share my enthusiasm when you come and stay with us in winter. Or any season, really. So if you would like to buy us a rivet of one, that would be so very kind.

Or perhaps you’d rather donate to the Elizabeth’s Legacy of Hope (Registered Charity Number 1141287), which is set up by a friend for children who've lost limbs through land mines, disease, etc, after Sarah’s daughter (my goddaughter) lost her leg in a horrible bus crash. Or James 1v27 Foundation (Registered Charity Number 1112730). It's a lovely very small orphanage in Goa, set up by my friend Jez, which I've spent time with the children there. This year their first graduating class celebrated getting jobs/education. v

Shoes

It would be really kind that if you're wearing stilettos, to bring something flat/with bigger heels for after the church (also there will be dancing!) It’s just that they make weird little puncture marks in the wooden floor at the hall, like the aftermath of a herd of very tiny sheep.
